New Topsail Inlet

New Topsail Inlet, located 40 kilometers northeast of Wilmington, separates Topsail Island, a 40-kilometer-long developed barrier island to the northeast, from Lea Island, a 2-kilometer-long barrier island to the southwest. Howards and Old Topsail Creeks provide access to the Atlantic Intracoastal Waterway. Land grants record the existence of New Topsail Inlet as early as 1726. The earliest coastal maps indicate the throat channel was 3 meters deep, sufficient to allow the passage of small schooners that serviced the coast. Maps indicate the inlet has migrated to the southwest at an average rate of 38 meters per year over the past 275 years.
