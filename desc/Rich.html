Rich Inlet

Rich Inlet is approximately 20 kilometers east of Wilmington, separating Coke Island, a 7-kilometer undeveloped barrier island to the north, from Figure Eight Island, a 9-kilometer, privately developed island to the south. The inlet forms the boundary between New Hanover and Pender Counties.


Rich Inlet drains an expansive marsh area where two large tidal creeks, Nixon and Green Channels, connect the inlet to the Atlantic Intracoastal Waterway. The inlet has been a relatively stable feature for the past two centuries. Its ultimate origin may be the ancestral channel of Pages Creek, which controlled the location of the inlet as sea level rose during the last several thousand years.
